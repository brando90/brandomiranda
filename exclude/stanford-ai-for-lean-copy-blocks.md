## Short Public Description

Stanford AI for Lean is a student-led research community at Stanford focused on AI-assisted theorem proving, Lean 4, formal verification, and trustworthy mathematical reasoning. The group has roughly 90 Discord members, meets weekly with about 12-15 regular attendees, and connects students to research directions around Lean 4, autoformalization, formal proof search, and verified code generation in the orbit of Prof. Sanmi Koyejo's Stanford Trustworthy AI Research group.

## OSE / ASSU Organization Description

Stanford AI for Lean is an academic and technical student community for Stanford students interested in AI-assisted theorem proving, Lean 4, formal verification, programming languages, and mathematical reasoning. We host weekly reading-group and working-session meetings, introduce students to Lean and formal methods, and help students engage with current research on trustworthy AI and verified reasoning. The group is open to interested Stanford students and currently has roughly 90 Discord members, with about 12-15 students attending weekly meetings.

## Room Request Copy

Stanford AI for Lean requests a weekly classroom or seminar room for a student research meeting on AI-assisted theorem proving and formal verification in Lean 4. Expected attendance is 12-15 students, with occasional growth for guest speakers or demos. A suitable room would have seating for 20-25 people, a projector or display, reliable Wi-Fi, and a whiteboard. The meeting format is technical but low-impact: paper discussion, Lean tutorials, project updates, and small-group work.

## Funding Request Copy

Funding will support recurring student programming for Stanford AI for Lean, a student-led research community focused on Lean 4, AI-assisted theorem proving, formal verification, and trustworthy mathematical reasoning. Funds will be used for weekly meeting food, onboarding materials, guest-speaker logistics, and small workshop or demo-night costs. The programming benefits Stanford students by making a fast-growing technical area more accessible and by helping students build research skills in formal methods, AI, programming languages, and mathematical reasoning.
