## Research Summary

My research develops trustworthy AI methods for mathematical reasoning, formal verification, and robust evaluation of foundation models. A central thread of my current work is AI for formal mathematics: building Lean 4 theorem-proving benchmarks, autoformalization methods, and end-to-end verification workflows that let AI-generated software and mathematical claims be checked against formal proof obligations rather than only informal tests. I also work on contamination-resistant evaluation of LLM mathematical reasoning and data-centric machine learning methods for understanding foundation and frontier models.

This agenda connects rigorous machine reasoning with practical reliability. My recent projects include VeriBench, an end-to-end Lean 4 benchmark for evaluating AI-generated code with formal verification proofs; VeriBench-FTP, a Lean 4 formal theorem-proving benchmark for code verification; Putnam-AXIOM, a benchmark for higher-level mathematical reasoning; and Pantograph, a machine-to-machine interface for Lean 4 theorem proving and data extraction. Across these projects, my goal is to make AI systems more measurable, verifiable, and useful for high-stakes reasoning tasks.
